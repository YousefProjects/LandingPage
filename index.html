Design Process

Phase 1: Discovery

The first step of the design process is researching and planning also known as ‚ÄúDiscovery‚Äù. Here, we do market research to know the market size and competitors, and user research to understand the user goals, needs, and pain points. I also t. and finally write the project goals.

Phase 2: Prototype

After the discovery phase, we can start visualizing the experience from beginning to end. I will begin to identify the main concept and ensure it is consistent with the project objectives. Then start designing the project structure, then the aesthetic aspect. Basically, the prototype phase is where the design comes to life.

Phase 3: Build

In this phase, the developers will take the design and build it. Here we will see the project become fully interactive. My role here is to think from the developers' perspective. I will provide a design system, which will ensure consistency across the final design and help the developers create new templates of any size.

Phase 4: Live

Nothing beats watching real users carry out natural interactions to learn how to improve your design. In this phase, the project is live. This will give us the chance to make sure the design is working well. Here we watch users interact and get their feedback to improve the design.
